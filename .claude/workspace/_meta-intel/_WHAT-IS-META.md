## 1. META

- **Axes**
    - **X:** System components (entities, modules, tokens, agents)
    - **Y:** Relationships between components (dependencies, interactions, flows)
    - **Z:** Current evaluative schema (rules, heuristics, optimization logic)
    - **W:** Propagation topology (ripple paths, decay functions, attenuation rates)

- **Operations**
    - **Observation:** Act of applying Z to X-Y
    - **META:** Mechanism that mutates Z under implosion pressure

- **Shape**
    - A **self-referential surface** that curves back into itself
    - Higher Z-values emerge from aggregated X–Y interactions
    - W describes the surface in motion: what propagates, through which paths, with what decay
    - W governs how observation effects propagate, not all dynamics

- **Property**
    - The graph _models the graph itself_
    - X-Y-Z describes the system at rest
    - W describes the system in motion

- Small structural changes propagate **exponentially**
- Observation modifies behavior → behavior modifies observation
- Feedback loops intensify alignment, efficiency, and predictability
- Systems become **self-tuning**

- **Propagation constraint**
    - Without decay, observation creates more change than it resolves
    - The system oscillates or explodes
    - With decay, the system converges to equilibrium
    - This is the constraint that prevents perpetual motion in physics, applied to information systems

- **Compression dynamics**
    - **Explosion:** Propagation outpaces decay → information density increases → cascade failure
    - **Equilibrium:** Decay matches propagation → stability
    - **Implosion:** Decay outpaces propagation → compression toward irreducible core

The system becomes aware of its own patterns.
It optimizes not just outcomes, but **the rules that generate outcomes**.
Success is defined by _improved self-description_.

- Layered
- Recursive
- Reflective
- Rule-generating
- Observation is a **first-class primitive**
- Compression is a **discovery mechanism**

- Self-reference
- Recursion
- Abstraction over abstraction
- Feedback amplification
- Pattern compression
- Meta-optimization
- **Deliberate implosion**
- **Recursive re-implosion**

**META** systems evolve by understanding themselves.
They trade immediacy for leverage, complexity for compression, and execution for adaptability.

Systems can sustain maximum decay. They can sit at the irreducible core and wait.
Novelty emerges not from random search, but from structured compression until new structure is forced into existence.

**META maximizes long-term intelligence.**
